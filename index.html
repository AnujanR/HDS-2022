PROJECT HOTDOG SHOWDOWN !!

For the past eight years, ‘Hot Dog Showdown’ has been the signature project of the Rotaract Club of Informatics Institute of Technology. Hot Dog Showdown is a food eating challenge that started within IIT more than a decade ago, since then the initiative has reached thousands of spectators and participants over the years. The pure nature of the project and its impact is what makes Hot Dog Showdown an award winning initiative within the Rotaract community.

All funds raised by ‘Hot Dog Showdown’ would go toward the club’s main community service initiative, ‘E-Mithudama’, under which the club builds and donates computer laboratories to children in rural areas with limited learning resources. We aspire to give our event partners a platform in which they can represent themselves across thousands of Roratactors, Interactors and the general public.

Since 2013

Since 2013, Hot Dog Showdown (HDS) has been an annual project of the Rotaract club of IIT. Over the years the Rotaract District 3220 has awarded HDS with their ‘Best Public Relations Effort’ for several years and we have expanded the project with the help of our members and partnerships to make an impact bigger than the year before. HDS has evolved into much more than a hotdog eating competition; it has become a platform for the Rotaract club of IIT to reach a much wider audience within the country.

MORE FUN

HDS was last held in 2020 since then the various challenges prevented us from hosting Hot Dog Showdown, in 2022 HDS hopes to make a return after 2 years since its last debut. This year we strive to make our initiative more impactful than the years before. Similar to years’ before HDS 2022 will be based around a hotdog eating competition but the event will feature additions such as carnival games, musical performances, a motor show,food and shopping stalls and much more.

WHY WE DO IT?

E-MITHUDAMA

Rotaract Club of IIT initiated “E-MIthudama” a community service project based around the core objective of helping school children from rural, underprivileged areas of the country- to support and uplift their computer literacy, delivering to the younger generation of our country with the touch of advance technology that the world is currently experiencing

The project was inspired by the opportunity that could be created by partnering up with Unilever Sri Lanka Limited, the basic ideology of the project being to use laptops and desktops that are left to be dumped, to build “X-Labs” in four underprivileged schools each year. Each device is tested to check if they are in a working condition, filtered and then given a proper clean up and they are sealed, to be transported to selected schools.Once in every 4 years the schools will have their labs renewed with new devices, adding value to the project due to its focus on sustainability.

Once the lab is set up, and it is open to be used, sessions on basic computing are conducted- how to use a computer, Microsoft Office etc. so the students are given an introductory path for guidance in their computer literacy.Apart from which a separate session is conducted to the mentors and teachers of these students to get ahead with their teaching and tutoring.

“E- Mithudama” was born in the 9th charter year of the Rotaract Club of IIT, since which, it has grown to be one of RACIIT’s ground-breaking signature community service projects, a promising project that brings light to different communities all around the nation, so on a day in future we can rise ourselves up as one, cherishing on the impact we have brought for others.
